Cisco Challenge 2017

by taiyang Jan 14th, 2017

Overview

It is currently hard to escape the news and ignore the major refugee crisis that is occurring in our world. We address the safety in refugee camps. It is a big concern, especially for women. Many reports show the high instances of sexual harassment, degrading body searches, and rape that female victims face. Our goal is to create a sense of community that values security and watching each otherâ€™s backs. Our second goal is to create a device that helps women rest with a peace of mind at night.

Merketing

Our market, which is low income females, specifically those in refugee camps, have access to smart phones and Wi-Fi, but may have to share the phone with family members. Secondly, having an app running for the entire night while a person is sleeping does not make sense economically since the phone is shared and used by other and the battery life may be limited. Another key difference is that many of these apps, such as Companion, bSafe, and Watch Over Me, require you to previously designate the friends and family that will be notified if you are unsafe.

Concept

We propose a broadcast distress signal instead of designating contacts. Users will not have to worry about removing designating contacts once they move. Sending a broadcast text message to all those in the vicinity helps create a digital vigilante and create a sense of security for the community. Proposed advertising and promotion strategy is to target not only those women in refugee camps, but women in general who live alone and would like to feel more secure at night.

Further

This same idea could also be used in other communal camping situations such as children summer camps or music festivals. In the future, the use case is expanded then we would also advertise to summer camps, festival organizers. Cities with a high percentage of single women living alone would also be targeted for advertising.
