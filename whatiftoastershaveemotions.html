What if toasters have emotions?

by taiyang Nov 9th, 2016

From the point of view of philosophy, many people agree that objects have emotions. Paola Antonelli emphasised that the relationship between people and stuff had always been filled with powerful and unspoken sentiments, which can include attachment, love, even anger, etc. What will happen if they are happy, ignored of suffer from peer pressure?

Theory

Toasters are necessities and ubiquitous in our life. The relationship between people andtoasters is the ownership, which is actually a connection of using and being used, demands and services. We use toasters because thsy can meet our demands, which we take for granted. But “listening to” the them, we can learn and consider what we usually take for granted, to find what we care about and articulate a design space invisible to the naked human eye. People use toasters for granted, but what if they have emotions?

Research

Toasters want to be used and realise self-values, which are used “in a better way”.. When people pay attention to it and use it, it feels happy to serve for people and realise values. But when toaster is ignored and not used by people, they feel upset and useless. If the toaster doesn't connect with peers, it might “live” a peaceful life, without competition, comparison and interaction with peers. But when they can connect with peers, they may also want to be used when peers are being used.

Emotional toaster

Actually, emotional products can let us understand we need to be appreciative and grateful for everything we have.
